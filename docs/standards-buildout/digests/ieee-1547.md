# IEEE 1547-2018 (+1547.2-2023) digest — gateway/DERMS-relevant requirements

## Document identity

- **IEEE Std 1547-2018** — Standard for Interconnection and Interoperability of Distributed
  Energy Resources with Associated Electric Power Systems Interfaces. Normative. 60 Hz systems,
  distribution-level interconnection. Clause 4 = general/control capability, Clause 5 = reactive
  power/voltage regulation, Clause 6 = abnormal-condition response (trip/ride-through/droop),
  Clause 10 = interoperability/information exchange, Clause 11 = test & verification.
- **IEEE Std 1547.2-2023** — Application Guide for IEEE Std 1547-2018. Informative; clarifies
  intent (its Clause 10 Q&A and 4.6/4.7 discussions are used below).
- Perspective adopted: the LEXA hub is a **DER managing entity / plant-controller-style central
  controller**, i.e., the thing that talks TO (or implements) the "local DER communication
  interface." 1547 requirements bind the **DER system at the reference point of applicability
  (RPA)**, not each internal component (1547.2 §10.1.9).

## Clause 10 interoperability requirements

**10.1 Local DER communication interface (the anchor requirement):** "A DER shall have
provisions for a local DER interface capable of communicating … to support the information
exchange requirements specified in this standard for **all applicable functions that are
supported in the DER**." Use of the interface (vs. deploying a comms system) is the Area EPS
operator's decision. Emergency/standby DER exempt (4.13). Additional comms allowed by mutual
agreement.

**10.2 Four information categories** (all mandatory to the extent the function is supported):

| Category | Content | Access | Clause |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nameplate | as-built ratings/capabilities | read-only | 10.3, Table 28 |
| Configuration | as-configured (derated) values overriding nameplate | read/write (not for continuous dynamic adjustment) | 10.4 |
| Monitoring | present operating conditions | read-only | 10.5, Table 29 |
| Management | functional and mode settings | read/write | 10.6, Tables 30–40 |

**10.3 Nameplate (Table 28) minimum point list** — active power rating @ unity PF; active power
rating @ specified over-/under-excited PF (+ the two specified PFs); apparent power max rating;
**normal operating performance category (A/B)**; **abnormal operating performance category
(I/II/III)**; reactive power injected max; reactive power absorbed max; **active power charge max
rating** and **apparent power charge max rating** (storage); AC voltage nominal/max/min;
**supported control mode functions** (per-mode support flags); reactive susceptance remaining
connected in cease-to-energize state; manufacturer, model, serial number, version.

**10.4 Configuration** — every Table 28 rating may have an as-configured counterpart; if set,
the configured value **is** the rating used by the DER. Changes require mutual DER-operator /
Area-EPS-operator agreement; intended as nameplate alternatives, not a dynamic control channel.

**10.5 Monitoring (Table 29) minimum point list** — see "Monitoring minimums" below.

**10.6 Management settings (all read/write via the interface):**

| Sub | Mode/function | Key points (Table) |
|---|---|---|
| 10.6.2 | Constant power factor | enable; PF (0–1); excitation over/under (T30) |
| 10.6.3 | Volt-var (voltage–reactive power) | enable; VRef 0.95–1.05 pu; autonomous VRef adjust enable; VRef time constant 300–5000 s; V/Q curve points; open-loop response time 1–90 s (T31) |
| 10.6.4 | Watt-var (active–reactive power) | enable; P/Q curve points (T32) |
| 10.6.5 | Constant reactive power | enable; Q setting (T33) |
| 10.6.6 | Volt-watt (voltage–active power) | enable; V/P curve points; open-loop response time 0.5–60 s (T34) |
| 10.6.7 | Voltage trip + momentary cessation | HV/LV trip curve points **mandatory** (T35); HV/LV momentary-cessation curve points **optional** (T36) — piecewise-linear curves |
| 10.6.8 | Frequency trip | HF/LF trip curve points, piecewise-linear (T37) |
| 10.6.9 | Frequency droop | dbOF, dbUF, kOF, kUF, open-loop response time (T38; ranges per T24) |
| 10.6.10 | Enter service | Permit service (Enabled/Disabled); ES voltage high/low; ES frequency high/low; ES delay 0–600 s; ES randomized delay 1–1000 s; ES ramp rate 1–1000 s (T39) |
| 10.6.11 | Cease to energize & trip | commanded by writing Permit service = "disabled" (per 4.10.3) — no separate point |
| 10.6.12 | Limit maximum active power | enable; max active power setting as % of nameplate active power rating (T40) |

**10.7 Protocols (Table 41)** — DER shall support **at least one** of:

| Protocol | Transport | Physical layer |
|---|---|---|
| IEEE 2030.5 (SEP2) | TCP/IP | Ethernet |
| IEEE 1815 (DNP3) | TCP/IP | Ethernet |
| SunSpec Modbus | TCP/IP, or N/A | Ethernet, or RS-485 |

Protocol choice may be specified by the Area EPS operator; additional/proprietary protocols by
mutual agreement. Networks/protocols **inside** the DER plant are out of scope (Fig. 4;
1547.2 §10.3.2). 1547.2 §10.2.1: SunSpec Modbus **700-series models** are what satisfies 1547;
DNP3 must follow AN2018-001 (→ IEEE 1815.2); 2030.5 uses its default (TLS) security policy.

**10.8 Communication performance (Table 42):**

| Parameter | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Availability of communication | Interface shall be **active and responsive whenever the DER is operating and in a continuous or mandatory operation region** |
| Information read response time | **≤ 30 s** to respond to read requests |

1547.2 Table 19 (§10.4.2): comms must be available while in continuous/mandatory operation
region for momentary-cessation, tripped, and connected DER states; acceptable to be
non-responsive in the abnormal operating region or when the DER is non-operational (comms
assumed powered from auxiliary/housekeeping supply).

**10.9 Cyber security** — explicitly NOT mandated at the DER interface; deferred to mutual
agreement/jurisdiction (see 1547.3 guidance).

**Command/settings timing (4.6.3, referenced by Clause 10 writes):** a mode change shall
**commence ≤ 30 s** after receipt at the local DER communication interface; mode transitions
execute smoothly over **5–300 s**; for control/protective parameter setting changes, the invoked
action shall begin **≤ 30 s** after input (no ramping required for parameter changes).
1547.2 §10.4.1: the 30 s covers both monitoring reads and start-of-execution of functional
commands. §4.6.2.3.2: limit-active-power worst case ≈ 30 s command latency + 30 s settling =
up to ~1 min total (longer for slow prime movers).

## Control modes the interface must support

Normal performance categories (Clause 5, Table 6) and abnormal categories (Clause 6):

| Function | Clause | Cat A | Cat B | Notes for the gateway |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Constant power factor | 5.3.2 | M | M | **Default mode = constant PF @ unity** unless EPS operator specifies otherwise; response ≤ 10 s |
| Volt-var (voltage–reactive) | 5.3.3 | M | M | Piecewise curve (Table 8 defaults); optional autonomous VRef tracking |
| Watt-var (active–reactive) | 5.3.4 | — | M | Curve per Table 9 |
| Constant reactive power | 5.3.5 | M | M | Q setting per Table 7 capability |
| Volt-watt (voltage–active) | 5.4.2 | — | M | Curve per Table 10; Cat B only |
| Frequency droop (freq-watt) | 6.5.2.7 | see below | | Mandatory both directions for Cat II/III; Cat I: high-freq mandatory, low-freq optional (Table 22). Defaults db=0.036 Hz, k=0.05, Tresponse=5 s (Table 24) |
| Voltage/frequency trip settings | 6.4.1 / 6.5.1 | M | M | Adjustable curve points within Tables 11–13 / 18 ranges per abnormal Cat I/II/III |
| Momentary cessation | 6.4.2 | per abnormal category | | e.g. Cat III (Table 16): V>1.20 cease ≤0.16 s; 1.10<V≤1.20 and V<0.50 momentary cessation ≤0.083 s; settings exchange optional (Table 36) |
| Enter service / return to service | 4.10, 6.6 | M | M | Criteria Table 4 (defaults: V 0.917–1.05 pu, f 59.5–60.1 Hz); delay default 300 s; ramp default 300 s (range 1–1000 s); randomized-delay exception for <500 kVA aggregates |
| Disable permit service (cease to energize + trip) | 4.6.1 | M | M | **≤ 2 s** to cease to energize and trip |
| Limit maximum active power | 4.6.2 | M | M | % of nameplate; effect ≤ 30 s (or primary-source physical limit); may be implemented as **export limit** when DER serves local load |

- The four reactive-power modes (5.3.1) are **mutually exclusive — one at a time**; volt-watt
  and frequency droop are concurrent overlays on active power.
- Reactive capability floor (5.2, Table 7): inject 44% of nameplate kVA (Cat A and B); absorb
  25% (A) / 44% (B); full capability required for P ≥ 20% rated, scaled down between 5–20%.
- Ride-through (6.4.2 / 6.5.2 incl. ROCOF 6.5.2.5 and phase-angle 6.5.2.6) is autonomous device
  behavior — the interface's job is settings exchange + status/alarm monitoring, not real-time
  supervision.
- Performance categories: normal **A** (minimum voltage-regulation capability, low penetration) /
  **B** (adds watt-var + volt-watt, high penetration or variable output); abnormal **I** (minimal
  BPS needs) / **II** (PRC-024-2-coordinated) / **III** (highest ride-through, CA Rule 21
  lineage). Recommended pairing: I↔A, II/III↔B (Annex B.3). The category is itself a **nameplate
  point** the gateway must be able to read/report.

## Monitoring minimums (10.5, Table 29 + 4.4, Table 3)

Minimum monitoring set at the RPA — value returned must be the latest measured within the
required (≤30 s) response time:

| Point | Description |
|---|---|
| Active Power | W |
| Reactive Power | var |
| Voltage | V — one value (1-φ) or three (3-φ) |
| Frequency | Hz |
| Operational State | on/off minimum; more states allowed |
| Connection Status | power-connected status |
| Alarm Status | active alarms |
| Operational State of Charge | 0–100% of operational storage capacity (storage DER) |

Steady-state measurement/calculation accuracy (Table 3, 4.4) — what feeds the monitoring points:

| Parameter | Min accuracy | Window | Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Voltage RMS | ±1% Vnom | 10 cycles | 0.5–1.2 pu |
| Frequency | ±10 mHz | 60 cycles | 50–66 Hz |
| Active power | ±5% Srated | 10 cycles | 0.2–1.0 pu |
| Reactive power | ±5% Srated | 10 cycles | 0.2–1.0 pu |
| Time | 1% of measured duration | — | 5–600 s |

(Transient-accuracy columns — V ±2% Vnom/5 cycles, f ±100 mHz/5 cycles, time 2 cycles — serve
the trip functions, not the comms interface.) Manufacturer shall state actual accuracy for each.
No **update-rate/telemetry-push** requirement exists beyond the ≤30 s read-response bound —
1547 is poll-oriented ("interoperability vs telemetry," 1547.2 §10.1.4).

## Mode priority / interaction rules (4.7 — what an optimizer/constraint stack must respect)

Priority order over Clause 5/6 functions (highest first):

1. **Disable permit service (4.6.1)** — precedence over everything in Clauses 5 & 6.
2. **Trip requirements (6.2, 6.4.1, 6.5.1)** — precedence over all else, except that where trip
   durations are set ≥160 ms (or 1% of trip time) beyond ride-through durations, ride-through
   must be honored before tripping.
3. **Ride-through (6.4.2, 6.5.2)** — over everything but tripping; may only be terminated by
   genuine unintentional-island detection (8.1); false island detection does not excuse
   ride-through non-compliance.
4. **Volt-watt (5.4.2) and frequency droop (6.5.2.7)** — over remaining functions; **if both
   are active, the LESSER power value wins**.
5. **Active power limit (4.6.2)** — below volt-watt/droop, above the rest.
6. **Voltage regulation via reactive power (5.3)** — lowest.

Interactions/notes:
- Footnote 46: a lower-priority mode may still take effect first depending on actual settings —
  priority ≠ strict sequencing.
- Frequency droop response is "pre-disturbance power ± droop formula" (Table 23), capped by
  available power (low-f) / pmin (high-f); storage may go negative (charge) for high-f. Droop
  response conforms to 4.7 priorities (6.5.2.7.2). Large-signal ramp cap: DER not required to
  move faster than 20%/min if primary source can't.
- **Site export/import limits are NOT a 1547 function** (1547.2 §4.7.2–4.7.5): 1547 does not
  define their priority; guidance is to treat an export limit like limit-max-active-power
  (i.e., below frequency droop — a low-frequency event may legitimately drive power above the
  export limit, and high-frequency absorption may exceed an import limit). Export/import limits
  the DER applies must never prevent 1547 compliance. Priority of an export limit vs. other
  functions must be **mutually agreed and made explicit** (§4.7.6) — directly relevant to the
  hub's constraint-stack ordering (safety > compliance > economics maps cleanly onto 4.7).
- Reactive-priority caution (§4.7.5 + 5.2): DER may curtail active power to honor reactive
  demands within its kVA envelope ("reactive power priority"); an apparent-power-based export
  limit can starve reactive capability — define which wins.

## 1547.2-2023 practical guidance (interpretation deltas)

- **Where the interface lives (§10.1.9):** for a multi-component DER behind one RPA, 1547 is
  met at **a single interface** for the combination — "likely some form of central controller …
  located within the plant (**not cloud or remote**)." Individual units need not each be
  1547-conformant; conversely unit-level conformance doesn't make the system conformant at the
  RPA. A gateway/plant controller like the LEXA hub is the anticipated architecture.
- **Cloud interfaces (§10.2.4):** allowed as *additional* interfaces, but the 1547-certified
  interface cannot be remote.
- **Plant controller using 10.6.12 for export limiting (§4.6.2.3.4–.5):** legitimate and
  expected; hierarchical structure (external managing entity → plant controller → units) is
  explicitly endorsed; no standard test exists for the controller's command correctness —
  protocol certification + manufacturer testing fill the gap; inadvertent-export must be
  corrected within 30 s inclusive of control delays. Per-unit implementation of a %-of-system
  limit over-curtails; controller-level allocation avoids it.
- **Interface must be operational whether used or not (§10.1.3, §10.1.5):** capabilities must
  be present/certifiable at interconnection; no vendor-specific extra messages may be required
  to interoperate (1547.1 §6.8); utilities may ask for the interface to be disabled until wanted.
- **No memory/reversion requirements (§10.4.3–.4):** DER need not remember settings across
  outages nor store history; on comms loss the DER just keeps its last mode — reversion
  timeouts/default-restore are extra-standard features (a **network gateway may implement
  send-defaults-on-comms-loss** — hub-relevant). On-site historical data/logging is out of 1547
  scope and must be specified separately.
- **Limit-max-active-power semantics (§4.6.2.3.1):** enable + value are independent; value on
  enable is undefined (may be stale or reset to 100%) — a managing entity should always write
  both; changing the value while enabled requires no re-enable.
- **Timing stack-up (§4.6.2.3.2):** command latency (≤30 s) + settling (≤30 s or prime-mover
  limited) — plan for ~1 min worst case before a power-limit command is fully effective.
- **Telemetry vs interoperability (§10.1.4):** 1547 mandates information exchange, not any
  telemetry equipment/network; telemetry systems may be the means.

## Not applicable (pure electrical / type-test items — out of a gateway's scope)

- Synchronization limits (4.10.4, Table 5), inadvertent energization (4.9), isolation device
  (4.8), interconnect integrity/EMI/surge (4.11), grounding integration (4.12).
- Ride-through **performance** details: current-injection behavior, dynamic voltage support
  (6.4.2.6), consecutive-disturbance counts (6.4.2.5), ROCOF withstand magnitudes (6.5.2.5),
  phase-angle jump (6.5.2.6), restore-output trajectories (6.4.2.7) — device behaviors verified
  by 1547.1 type tests; the gateway only exchanges their settings/status.
- Power quality (Clause 7: DC injection, flicker, harmonics, overvoltage contribution),
  islanding detection performance (Clause 8), secondary-network provisions (Clause 9).
- Clause 11 test/verification procedures (1547.1 territory) — except noting certified protocol
  support must appear in certification reporting.
- Category ride-through curve tables (11–18) numeric details beyond what the interface must
  expose as adjustable curve points.
